How to make Causa Rellena

Description

Causa is a best described as a sort of mini-casserole, with a top and bottom composed of mashed potato mixed with lime juice and aji amarillo (Peruvian yellow pepper), and a filling consisting of, well, virtually anything. Commonly the middle will be tuna, lobster, crab, octopus, shrimp, avocado, or chicken.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. golden potatoes (peeled)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • Filling of your choice (the recipe in the link uses tuna salad)

Steps

  1. Boil the potatoes, then drain. Pass boiled potatoes through ricer or masher and let cool to room temperature.
  2. Add potatoes to a large mixing bowl with olive oil, lime juice, aji amarillo paste. Stir to combine, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Tightly line a casserole dish with plastic wrap.
  4. Fill the bottom 1/3 of a casserole dish with half of the mashed potato mixture. Smooth over.
  5. Place a thin layer of sliced avocado on top of the bottom layer of the mashed potato mixture.
  6. Add the filling of your choice on top of the avocado, filling the casserole dish to about 2/3 full.
  7. Fill the casserole to the top with a second layer of the mashed potato mixture. Smooth over.
  8. Place plastic wrap over the top of the dish and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  9. Flip casserole dish upside down on to plate to un-mold causa. Remove plastic wrap.
  10. Serve cold and enjoy!
